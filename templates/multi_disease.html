Overall Risk Assessment

This risk prediction tool helps assess potential health concerns by analyzing various blood and health parameters. Early detection enables better decisions and healthier outcomes.

What Inputs Are Collected and Why?

  • Glucose: Measures blood sugar level; high values indicate diabetes risk.
  • Hemoglobin: Oxygen-carrying protein; low levels suggest anemia.
  • Platelets: Help with clotting; abnormal values may indicate disorders.
  • WBC: Elevated levels may indicate infection or inflammation.
  • RBC: Low RBCs can lead to fatigue or dizziness.
  • Hematocrit: Red blood cell %; key for hydration/anemia status.
  • MCV: Average size of red blood cells.
  • MCH: Hemoglobin amount per red cell.
  • MCHC: Hemoglobin concentration in red cells.
  • BMI: Body Mass Index; evaluates obesity-related risk factors.
  • HbA1c: Reflects average glucose levels over the past 3 months.

How Is Risk Predicted?

Our AI models are trained on real-world datasets to recognize early warning signs. We calculate patterns from your submitted values and determine if you're at low, medium, or high risk for chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, or kidney issues.
